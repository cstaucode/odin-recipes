Beef Enchiladas

What You Need:

Spice Mix:

  • 1 Tbs. onion powder
  • 1 Tbs. each cumin, paprika, and oregano
  • 1/2 - 1 tsp (or more! I use 1 tbs) cayenne pepper

Enchilada Sauce

  • 2 tbs. olive olive
  • 3 tbs flour
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 1/2 cups (375ml) tomato paste
    • Should be about 2 cans. Tomato sauce can be used instead.
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Beef

  • 1 tbs olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 onion
  • 1 lbs ground beef
  • 14oz (400g)black beans
  • 1 green pepper

Toppings

  • Tortillas
  • 1 1/2 cups cheese
  • cilantro

Directions:

  1. Mix together the spice ingredients and set aside
  2. Enchilada Sauce
    1. Heat oil in saucepan on medium heat. Add flour and stir continuously for 1 minute.
    2. Add 1/2 cup chicken broth and whisk immediately
    3. Add rest of the brother, tomato paste/sauce, salt, pepper, and 2 tbs spice mix.
    4. Increase the head to medium-high. Cook 3-5 minutes. Whisk regularly until thickens to like a syrup consistency.
  3. Enchilada Filling
    1. Heat the oil in a pan and throw in the garlic and onion
    2. Add beef and cook for a couple of minutes
    3. Add spice mix. Cook until browned.
    4. Add beans, 1/4 cup enchilada sauce, salt and pepper.
  4. Final Steps
    1. Pre-heat the oven to 180° C/350° F
    2. Smear some sauce on the bottom of the pan to prevent sliding
    3. Place filling on 1/3 of the tortilla
    4. Roll tortilla up and place it seam side down into the pan. Repeat.
    5. Sour sauce on top. Top with cheese and bake for 10 minutes covered and then 10 minutes uncovered.
    6. Garnish with cilantro
